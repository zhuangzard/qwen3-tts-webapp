## Why Investment Is Unlikely

1. **Debt structure is prohibitive.** Any new investment would be immediately consumed by the $25M high-interest debt, providing zero growth capital. No rational investor accepts this.

2. **No revenue to underwrite.** Without a revenue stream or clear path to commercialization, there is no basis for a valuation that supports investment.

3. **Insufficient team.** Five employees cannot execute a product commercialization strategy in the medical device space, which requires regulatory, engineering, clinical, and commercial capabilities.

4. **Founder age and succession risk.** A 73-year-old sole founder with no succession plan represents unacceptable key-person risk for institutional investors.

5. **14-year pattern.** In 14 years of operation, no investment has ever closed. Repeated claims of "investor interest" without execution is a strong predictor of continued non-execution.

## Scenarios

### Best Case (Probability: < 5%)
A strategic acquirer (large medical device company) acquires the IP portfolio at a modest valuation, pays off some debt, and retains key personnel. This would require the IP to have significant demonstrated value, which has not been established.

### Base Case (Probability: ~ 70%)
Company continues to deteriorate through Q1-Q2, faces creditor action in Q2-Q3, and ceases operations by summer 2026. Assets sold at distressed prices insufficient to cover secured debt. Unsecured creditors (including employees) recover little to nothing.

### Worst Case (Probability: ~ 25%)
Company lingers as a zombie entity through 2026, with CEO refusing to formally close. No salaries paid, no operations, but legal entity persists. This maximizes opportunity cost for remaining employees and delays resolution.
